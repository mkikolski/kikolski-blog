Biomeeting 2026 took place on 22-24th of May in Przesieka, Poland. It was a great opportunity to present my work and listen to some interesting talks, as usual, as it was my third Biomeeting. I feel kind of magical connection to this conference, both because of the great atmosphere and the fact that it was the first conference I've ever attended back during my Master's studies. This is also the first time I've been invited as a speaker and I used the chance to present my work on VR, EEG and eye tracking data in diagnosis and therapy of [hemispatial neglect](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC1757480/).

![Photo of me during presentation](/assets/images/biomeeting_presentation.JPG)
![Summary of my block's speakers](/assets/images/biomeeting_awards.JPG)

I've been working on this project for around a year now as a VR game developer and we've been through a lot (for example porting the whole solution to a totally different game engine as described in my [technical post](https://blog.kikolski.pl/2026/03/13/unity-grass-vr/)) and there's still some way to go. The presentation itself went quite smoothly but there's still some room for improvement - after stepping on the stage I felt the weight of being an invited speaker and I was a bit nervous (**note to myself**: maybe it would be a good idea to finally start preparing notes for the presentations at all).

That being said, I'd like to thank the organizers for the invitation and for putting together such a great conference. Over the past 3 years I've seen this conference grow and evolve and it's always a pleasure to be a part of it. This year's edition was no exception - the talks were interesting, the atmosphere was great and it wouldn't be possible without the hard work of this and past year's organizing commitees. Specifically, I'd like to thank [Ignacy](https://www.linkedin.com/in/ignacy-berent-74b355278/?locale=pl) - the head of this year's commitee that really went above and beyond to make this conference happen.
